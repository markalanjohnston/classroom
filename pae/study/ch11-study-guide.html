Quick Study Tips
  • Electricity: Flow of electrons through conductors
  • Good conductors: Copper, Aluminum, Gold, Steel
  • Good insulators: Rubber, Plastic, Paper
  • Static electricity: Lightning is the best example
  • Generators use magnetism to produce most electricity
  • Battery types: Secondary = Rechargeable, Primary = Disposable
  • Valence shell: Outer shell of an atom
  • Four characteristics: Voltage (V), Current (I), Resistance (R), Power (P)
  • Series circuits: One path for current flow
  • Parallel circuits: Multiple paths for current flow, voltage equal across loads
  • Ohm's Law: V = I × R (if R constant, V up means I up)
  • Diodes: Allow current flow in only ONE direction
  • Transistors: Solid-state switches with no moving parts
  • Capacitors: Store and discharge charge quickly, two plates separated by dielectric
  • Ceramic disc & electrolytic = Capacitors (not resistors)
  • Sensors: Send signals based on environmental conditions
  • Motors: Convert electrical energy to mechanical energy
  • LEDs: Efficient, low-cost, long-lasting, use DC
  • CFLs: Use 75% less energy than incandescent
  • Incandescent bulbs: Tungsten filament, oxygen removed
  • Neon lamps = Gas discharge lamps
  • Closed systems: Have built-in feedback
  • Open systems: No built-in feedback, need manual adjustment
  • False: Potentiometer does NOT control current direction (that's a diode)
  • False: Diodes do NOT store charge (capacitors do)
  • False: Closed systems do NOT rely on OUTSIDE feedback
