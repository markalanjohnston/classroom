Quick Study Tips
  • Four material categories: Metals, Ceramics, Polymers, Composites
  • Metals: Inorganic, good conductors of heat & electricity
  • Ceramics: Inorganic, brittle, good insulators
  • Composites: Utilize advantages of several materials
  • Stress = Force applied; Strain = Deformation
  • Radiography tests use x-rays; Ultrasonic tests use sound waves
  • Graphene is 1 atom thick; Nanowire is 1-60 nm wide
  • ASTM creates standards for materials tests (NOT Materials Information Society)
  • Materials have DIFFERENT properties at the nanoscale
  • Thermal resistance is the reciprocal of thermal conductivity
