Week 4 (8/25 - 8/29)
DayIn Class Plan
Mon 8/25What is Engineering Design?
  • Slides overview: Engineering design as an iterative process
  • Discussion: Everyday examples of design around us
  • Activity: Intro to bridge challenge — explain problem (span 24″ between desks, must hold weight, flat top for loading)
  • Groups form and start discussing initial thoughts
Tue 8/26Defining the Problem
  • Mini-lesson: Problem statements, criteria & constraints
  • Groups: Write their bridge problem statement + list criteria (must span 24″, support weight, flat deck, limited cardboard/glue/tape) + constraints (materials, time, size)
  • Start sketching rough concepts (stick figures/labels, not formal drawings)
Wed 8/27Ideation & Brainstorming
  • ½ class: Vocabulary game (Ch. 3)
  • ½ class: Brainstorming session — groups generate as many bridge concepts as possible (truss, beam, arch-like forms, layering cardboard)
  • Groups record all ideas (sketches/notes)
Thu 8/28Evaluating & Selecting Ideas
  • Mini-lesson: How engineers evaluate ideas with scoring matrices
  • Groups: Rank their brainstormed bridge designs on strength, weight, ease of building, materials use
  • Choose one final design and sketch it larger with labels
Fri 8/29Chapter 3 Vocabulary Quiz
  • Quiz: Vocabulary terms from Ch. 3 (brainstorming, prototype, CAD, criteria, constraints, iterative, etc.)
  • Remaining time: Teams refine bridge sketches and begin prep for build next week
Week 5 (9/2 - 9/5)
DayIn Class Plan
Tue 9/2Building Prototypes
  • Mini-lesson: Prototypes & why engineers build them
  • Groups begin building cardboard bridges (limited supplies)
  • End of class: Record progress in notebook/sketch
Wed 9/3Continue Build & Prep for Testing
  • Groups continue bridge construction
  • Teams finalize design for testing (flat deck reinforced, taped joints, etc.)
  • Optional mini-lesson: Safety and fairness in testing (weights, process)
Thu 9/4Testing & Design Improvement
  • Bridge testing day: span 24″ opening, add weights gradually until failure
  • Record test results (weight supported, observations)
  • Group reflection: What worked? What failed? How would you improve it?
  • Time permitting: Sketch a quick "improved" bridge concept
Fri 9/5Chapter 3 Concepts Quiz
  • Quiz: Covers engineering design process, criteria/constraints, ideation, prototypes, testing, teamwork, ethics, engineering notebooks
  • Format: Multiple choice, matching, and short answer
  • Wrap-up discussion: Why iteration matters in engineering
