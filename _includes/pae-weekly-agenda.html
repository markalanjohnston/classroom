Week 8 (9/22 - 9/26): Chapter 6 - Communicating Solutions Current Week
DayIn Class Plan
Mon 9/22 Why Communicate Solutions? & Types of Drawings
Objective: We will explain the importance of clear engineering drawings in industry.
Unit: Chapter 6 – Communicating Solutions
  • Introduce the importance of clear engineering drawings in industry
  • Cover working drawings (detail, assembly, schematic)
  • Compare with real-world examples (blueprints, circuit diagrams)
  • Activity: Students sketch a quick "detail drawing" of a simple classroom object
Tue 9/23 Drawing Classifications & Line Types
Objective: We will identify different drawing classifications and line types.
  • Explain orthographic drawings (one-, two-, three-view)
  • Cover pictorial drawings (isometric, oblique, perspective)
  • Teach five primary line types (border, object, hidden, center, construction)
  • Practice: "Line type scavenger hunt" — students label examples in handouts or slides
Wed 9/24 Dimensioning Guidelines & Symbols
Objective: We will apply dimensioning basics and industry symbols.
  • Cover dimensioning basics: size, location, and shape dimensions
  • Teach difference between extension lines and dimension lines
  • Discuss industry standards (ANSI, ISO) and the role of symbols
  • Activity: Students dimension a simple orthographic drawing and add symbols
Thu 9/25 Applied Drawing Activities
Objective: We will complete orthographic and perspective drawing activities.
  • Do Activity 6-1 (Orthographic Drawings): students fill in missing views and dimension them
  • If time: start Activity 6-2 (Perspective Drawings)
  • Reflection questions (why multiple views, why perspective is useful)
Fri 9/26 Technical Drawing & Practice Test
Objective: We will demonstrate technical drawing skills and test knowledge.
Assessment: Technical Drawing (Mastery Grade) & Chapter 6 Practice Test (Understanding Grade)
  • Students turn in a technical drawing (graded for Mastery)
  • Take a Chapter 6 practice test (Understanding grade) — aligns with unit test format
  • Wrap-up discussion: Which type of drawing felt most natural? Which was hardest?
Week 7 (9/15 - 9/19)
DayIn Class Plan
Mon 9/15 Introduction to Researching Designs
Objective: We will explain how engineers communicate design ideas using sketches.
Unit: Chapter 5 – Researching Designs
  • Warm-Up: "Why do engineers research before building?"
  • Review Ch. 5 objectives and key terms (sketch, rough sketch, refined sketch, library research, trade-off)
  • Teacher-led notes/discussion using slides (Idea Generation, Sketches)
  • Students practice thumbnail sketching of an everyday object (engineering notebook)
  • Closing: Share sketches with a partner
  • Homework: Read pp. 100–105 (Sketching section) in ch05.pdf
Tue 9/16 The Sketching Process
Objective: We will describe and apply the 4-step sketching process (visualize, block out, outline, detail).
  • Warm-Up: "What's the difference between a rough sketch and a final drawing?"
  • Slides: Sketching Process (Visualize → Block → Outline → Detail)
  • Guided activity: Students follow along while teacher demonstrates blocking shapes (boxes, cylinders, cones)
  • Independent work: Sketch and outline a simple product (e.g., chair, phone case)
  • Exit Ticket: Identify the 4 sketching steps in order
  • Homework: Study key terms: rough sketch, refined sketch, outline, detail
Wed 9/17 Research Methods
Objective: We will compare historical and experimental research methods.
  • Warm-Up: "Where might you look if you were designing a new backpack?"
  • Slides: Researching Ideas → Library Research, Field Visits, Reverse Engineering, Discussions with Others
  • Class activity: Small groups — each group lists examples of how engineers could use one method
  • Share-out discussion
  • Closing: Summary of historical research (library, field visits, reverse engineering, discussions)
  • Homework: Read pp. 110–120 in ch05.pdf (Researching Ideas)
Thu 9/18 Experimental Research & Trade-Offs
Objective: We will identify properties studied in experimental research and analyze trade-offs in design.
  • Warm-Up: "What's a trade-off you make in everyday life?"
  • Slides: Experimental Research → material testing, feasibility, production, effectiveness
  • Mini-lab: Students evaluate different materials for a cup design (paper, plastic, metal) → pros/cons chart
  • Slides: Trade-Offs & Matrix → show how engineers balance cost, effectiveness, appearance, environment
  • Students complete a trade-off chart comparing 3 transportation solutions (e.g., bike, car, bus)
  • Exit Ticket: Define "cost feasibility"
  • Homework: Study Chapter 5 Review Questions (Know & Understand)
Fri 9/19 Chapter 5 Quiz
Objective: We will demonstrate understanding of sketching, research methods, and trade-offs.
Assessment: Chapter 5 Quiz (Understanding Grade)
  • Warm-Up: Quick review game (Kahoot or flashcards)
  • Chapter 5 Quiz (Understanding Grade)
  • If time allows: Reflection prompt — "Which type of research (historical vs experimental) would you enjoy doing most as an engineer, and why?"
  • Homework: None (quiz closes Ch. 5)

Week 4 (8/25 - 8/29)
DayIn Class Plan
Mon 8/25What is Engineering Design?
  • Slides overview: Engineering design as an iterative process
  • Discussion: Everyday examples of design around us
  • Activity: Intro to bridge challenge — explain problem (span 24″ between desks, must hold weight, flat top for loading)
  • Groups form and start discussing initial thoughts
Tue 8/26Defining the Problem
  • Mini-lesson: Problem statements, criteria & constraints
  • Groups: Write their bridge problem statement + list criteria (must span 24″, support weight, flat deck, limited cardboard/glue/tape) + constraints (materials, time, size)
  • Start sketching rough concepts (stick figures/labels, not formal drawings)
Wed 8/27Ideation & Brainstorming
  • ½ class: Vocabulary game (Ch. 3)
  • ½ class: Brainstorming session — groups generate as many bridge concepts as possible (truss, beam, arch-like forms, layering cardboard)
  • Groups record all ideas (sketches/notes)
Thu 8/28Evaluating & Selecting Ideas
  • Mini-lesson: How engineers evaluate ideas with scoring matrices
  • Groups: Rank their brainstormed bridge designs on strength, weight, ease of building, materials use
  • Choose one final design and sketch it larger with labels
Fri 8/29Chapter 3 Vocabulary Quiz
  • Quiz: Vocabulary terms from Ch. 3 (brainstorming, prototype, CAD, criteria, constraints, iterative, etc.)
  • Remaining time: Teams refine bridge sketches and begin prep for build next week
