Assignment — Minecraft Lesson 5

Patterns, Functions & the Castle

Today’s Big Idea

For four lessons your for loops have had a counter named i — and you've ignored it every time. Today i becomes part of what you build: a coordinate, a height, a color picker. Then you'll add parameters to your functions so one definition can build towers anywhere, at any size — and compose several small helpers into one big build (a castle).

This is the final lesson in the Minecraft unit. It pulls together everything from Lessons 1–4 and pushes it one step further. The full self-paced lesson lives on the class website. Open the lesson page using the button at the bottom of this assignment, then come back here to submit.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Use the iteration variable i (and j in nested loops) inside the loop body to position blocks.
  • Use modulo (%) to make alternating patterns like checkerboards and crenellations.
  • Use abs(), max(), and the Pythagorean theorem to build pyramids and round structures.
  • Write a function that takes parameters — so you can call it at any position and size.
  • Combine several parameterized helpers into one bigger build by sharing a center.

Key Vocabulary

Term Meaning
Iteration variable The i in for i in range(N). Counts 0, 1, ..., N-1 — and can be used inside the loop body.
Modulo (%) The remainder after dividing. Turns numbers into repeating patterns — checkerboards, stripes, crenellations.
Distance from center How far a block is from a chosen point. max(abs, abs) gives a diamond; sqrt(dx²+dz²) gives a circle.
Parameter A value a function accepts when it's called. def round_tower(cx, cz, radius, height): takes four parameters.
Composition Calling several small functions from inside one bigger function — the castle = moat + wall + tower + dome at one shared center.

The Task

Create an original program that defines at least two parameterized helper functions and composes them at a shared center to make one coordinated build. Pick one of the challenge options on the lesson page (Twin-Tower Fortress, Volcano Island, Temple Complex, Custom Castle) or invent your own.

Minimum requirements:

  • Your program starts with a chat command (player.on_chat).
  • You define at least two helper functions with parameters.
  • You call each helper at least once. At least two helpers share a center (same cx, cz).
  • At least one helper uses i or j as a coordinate or in a height calculation — not just as a counter.
  • At least one helper uses modulo (%) or distance math (abs/max or Pythagorean).
  • Your build covers at least a 20×20 area or is 10+ blocks tall.
  • Your final program is different from the Step 8 castle in the lesson.

Reflection Questions

Answer all five. Aim for 1–2 sentences each. This is the longest reflection of the unit — show that you understand why parameterized helpers let you build big things from small pieces, not just that the code ran.

  1. What did your program build, and which helpers did you compose to make it?
  2. Name one place in your code where the iteration variable (i or j) was used as a coordinate or in math — not just as a counter.
  3. Did you use modulo, distance math, or both? What pattern or shape did the math create?
  4. Pick one of your helper functions and explain its parameters — what does each one control?
  5. If a classmate wanted to move your whole build to a new spot, what would they have to change? (Hopefully a small answer.)

Tips for a Strong Submission

  • Don't write code from scratch — remix. Copy the helpers from Step 8 of the lesson, tweak the numbers, then add a helper of your own.
  • Use the full-screen screenshot tool so your code is easy to read — Win+Shift+S works well.
  • Builds are big — fly up in creative mode and take a top-down or aerial shot so the whole shape is visible.
  • In your reflection, name specific values — e.g., "round_tower(30, 0, 5, 14, STONE_BRICKS, True) made a hollow stone tower of radius 5 and height 14" — not just "I called the tower function."
  • Stretch goals to brag about in your reflection: a list indexed by modulo, calling a helper in a for loop at multiple positions, adding mobs.spawn() inside your structure, or composing three or more helpers at one center.
