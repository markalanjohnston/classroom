Assignment — Minecraft Lesson 4

Filling Spaces & Spawning Mobs

Today’s Big Idea

In Lesson 3 you used the Builder to draw walls and lines in just a few commands. Today you'll go one step further: blocks.fill() creates a whole 3D region — a room, an arena, a pond — in one line. Then mobs.spawn() brings the space to life with sheep, fish, zombies, or whatever fits your scene.

The whole self-paced lesson — vocabulary, starter code, predict prompts, debugging examples, and the challenge — lives on the class website. Open the lesson page using the button at the bottom of this assignment, then come back here to submit when you're done.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this lesson, you should be able to:

  • Explain when blocks.fill() is a better tool than the Agent or the Builder.
  • Use two positions as opposite corners of a 3D rectangular region.
  • Choose intentionally between FillOperation.REPLACE, HOLLOW, and OUTLINE.
  • Use mobs.spawn() to place a mob at a specific or random position.
  • Combine a for loop with randpos() to populate a space with many mobs at once.
  • Recognize and fix two common bugs: a misindented loop body and a wrong Y value.

Key Vocabulary

Term Meaning
blocks.fill() Fills every block in a 3D box with the block type you choose.
Opposite corners The two positions you give fill() — one corner of the box and the corner farthest from it.
FillOperation Tells fill() how to fill: REPLACE, HOLLOW, OUTLINE, KEEP, or DESTROY.
mobs.spawn() Creates a mob (sheep, cow, fish, zombie, …) at a given position.
randpos() Returns a random position inside the box between two corners — great for spreading mobs around.

The Task

Create an original program that uses blocks.fill() and mobs.spawn() to build a habitat, arena, pond, aquarium, or scene of your own design. Your final program must be different from the starter examples on the lesson page.

Minimum requirements:

  • Your program starts with an event — either a chat command (player.on_chat) or an item interaction (player.on_item_interacted).
  • Uses blocks.fill() at least twice.
  • Uses at least two different block types.
  • Uses mobs.spawn() at least once.
  • Uses a for loop to spawn multiple mobs.
  • Uses randpos(...) or carefully chosen coordinates so the mobs end up inside the structure.
  • The final result is a habitat, arena, pond, aquarium, or other enclosed scene.

Reflection Questions

Answer all five. Aim for 1–2 sentences each — show that you understand why blocks.fill() was the right tool for this job, not just that it worked.

  1. What did your program build?
  2. Which two positions did you use as opposite corners for your main blocks.fill()?
  3. Which fill operation did you use — REPLACE, HOLLOW, or OUTLINE — and why?
  4. How did your loop affect the number of mobs that spawned?
  5. Why would this build be harder with the Agent or the Builder?

Tips for a Strong Submission

  • Use the full-screen screenshot tool so your code is easy to read — Win+Shift+S works well.
  • If your build is hard to see from one angle, fly up (creative mode) and take a top-down or aerial shot.
  • In your reflection, name the specific corners you used — e.g., "from pos(5, 0, -10) to pos(12, 7, 10)" — not just "I used two corners."
  • If you used FillOperation.OUTLINE, multiple mob types, or added an opening with an AIR fill, mention it — that's stretch-goal territory.
