AP CSP Review Lesson: Cybersecurity & Encryption

Today’s Goal: Review the most important cybersecurity concepts for the AP CSP exam, including symmetric vs. asymmetric encryption, digital certificates and HTTPS, multi-factor authentication, common threats, and personally identifiable information.

Today’s Agenda

TimeActivity
0–10 min Whole-Class Questioning: We will review key ideas out loud as a class.
10–30 min Guided Review: We will use the Cybersecurity & Encryption review page and discuss the major AP exam topics.
30–45 min Partner Flashcards: You and a partner will create 10 review flashcards.
45–55 min Flashcard Practice: Pairs will trade flashcards and quiz each other.
55–67 min 10-Question Quiz: Short answer, fill-in-the-blank, and matching. No multiple choice.
67–78 min Self-Grade and Corrections: We will go over answers, and you will correct your own quiz before turning it in.
78–88 min Kahoot Review Competition: Whole-class review game using today’s concepts.
88–90 min Exit Ticket: Quick reflection before leaving.

Key Topics We Are Reviewing

  • Encryption and decryption
  • Symmetric vs. asymmetric (public-key) encryption
  • Public and private keys — who uses which key, and when
  • Digital certificates and Certificate Authorities (CAs)
  • HTTPS — what it does and does not guarantee
  • Authentication and Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA)
  • The three authentication factor categories: KNOW, HAVE, ARE
  • Threat vocabulary: phishing, keylogger, rogue access point, virus, worm, malware
  • Personally Identifiable Information (PII) and aggregation
  • Common AP exam misconceptions

Partner Flashcard Requirements

  • One example, misconception, or AP-style warning when possible

Required terms:
