AP CSP Review Lesson: Internet Basics

Today’s Goal: Review the most important Internet concepts for the AP CSP exam, including packets, protocols, DNS, fault tolerance, and bandwidth vs. latency.

Today’s Agenda

TimeActivity
0–10 min Whole-Class Questioning: We will review key ideas out loud as a class.
10–30 min Guided Review: We will use the Internet Basics review page and discuss the major AP exam topics.
30–45 min Partner Flashcards: You and a partner will create 10 review flashcards.
45–55 min Flashcard Practice: Pairs will trade flashcards and quiz each other.
55–67 min 10-Question Quiz: Short answer and fill-in-the-blank. No multiple choice.
67–78 min Self-Grade and Corrections: We will go over answers, and you will correct your own quiz before turning it in.
78–88 min Kahoot Review Competition: Whole-class review game using today’s concepts.
88–90 min Exit Ticket: Quick reflection before leaving.

Key Topics We Are Reviewing

  • Internet vs. World Wide Web
  • Packet switching
  • Routing metadata
  • Fault tolerance and redundancy
  • Protocols: IP, TCP, UDP, HTTP/HTTPS, DNS
  • IPv4 vs. IPv6
  • Bandwidth vs. latency
  • Common AP exam misconceptions

Partner Flashcard Requirements

With your partner, create 10 flashcards. Each card should include:

  • A term or question on the front
  • A correct explanation on the back
  • One example, misconception, or AP-style warning when possible

Required terms:

  • Internet
  • World Wide Web
  • Packet
  • Metadata
  • TCP
  • IP
  • DNS
  • HTTP/HTTPS
  • Fault tolerance
  • Bandwidth vs. latency

Quiz Information

You will take a 10-question quiz with short answer and fill-in-the-blank questions. There will be no multiple choice.

After the quiz, we will review the answers together. You will grade your own quiz, make corrections, and turn it in.

Exit Ticket

At the end of class, answer:

  1. One thing I got wrong and now understand:
  2. One thing I still do not understand:
  3. One trap answer I will avoid on the AP test:

Class Expectations

  • Participate during whole-class questioning.
  • Work productively with your partner.
  • Take the quiz honestly.
  • Correct your mistakes carefully during self-grading.
  • Use Kahoot as review, not just guessing.
