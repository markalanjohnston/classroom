About this exam

A 40-question multiple-choice exam — a shorter, gentler version of the AP CSP exam, drawn entirely from the three exam-review units. Designed for roughly a 60-minute block.

  • Part 1 — The Internet (Q1–14): Internet vs. WWW, packet switching, fault tolerance, protocols (TCP/IP/UDP/HTTP/HTTPS/DNS), IPv4→IPv6, bandwidth vs. latency, open standards.
  • Part 2 — Cybersecurity & Encryption (Q15–27): symmetric vs. public-key encryption, certificates & HTTPS, MFA, threat vocabulary, PII aggregation.
  • Part 3 — Reading Pseudocode (Q28–40): assignment vs. equality, MOD, RANDOM, Boolean logic, IF/ELSE, REPEAT UNTIL, 1-indexed lists, FOR EACH, tracing, procedures.

Every distractor is built from a misconception explicitly addressed in the review lessons, and the answer letters are balanced across A/B/C/D. Scoring: 1 pt each — multiply the raw score by 2.5 for a percentage.
