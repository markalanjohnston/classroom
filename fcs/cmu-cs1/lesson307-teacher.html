Teacher Lesson Plan

Lesson 307: Unit 1 Creative Mini-Task

Lesson Overview

Course: Fundamentals of Computer Science
Lesson Code: 307
Lesson Title: Unit 1 Creative Mini-Task
Class Length: 90 minutes
Curriculum Source: CMU CS Academy CS1 – Unit 1 Creative Task

This lesson launches the first Creative Task in CMU CS Academy CS1. Students design and build an original program using the drawing and color concepts learned in Unit 1. The focus is on creativity, problem solving, and demonstrating understanding of Unit 1 skills such as shapes, coordinates, colors, and optional parameters. Students follow a structured design–code–reflect process.

Objectives

Students will:

  • Plan and design an original drawing-based program using Unit 1 concepts.
  • Create a working CMU CS Academy program that demonstrates use of shapes, colors, and properties.
  • Apply the design process to move from idea to finished product.
  • Reflect on their coding process and explain how Unit 1 concepts were used.

Materials & Setup

  • Student computers with internet access
  • CMU CS Academy login credentials
  • Student notes from Unit 1
  • Optional planning paper or digital planning document

Teacher Preparation:

  • Review Creative Task expectations and rubric
  • Be ready to show sample projects for inspiration
  • Prepare to model brainstorming and planning

Agenda (90 Minutes)

Time Activity

Teacher Moves (Scripted Talking Points + Demo Steps)

Introduction (10 minutes)

Talking Points

"Up to this point you have followed guided lessons and exercises. Today you get to create something of your own."

"This Creative Mini-Task is your chance to show what you can do with the skills from Unit 1."

"You may design a scene, picture, logo, or any visual idea you choose—as long as it uses Unit 1 concepts."

Emphasize:

  • There is not one "correct" answer
  • Creativity is encouraged
  • The project must still meet clear requirements

Review Expectations and Rubric (15 minutes)

Explain:

"Your project will be graded using a rubric. You are not only graded on code, but also on planning and reflection."

Walk through rubric categories:

  • Program Design
  • Program Development
  • Reflection

Model what a strong project includes:

  • Multiple shapes
  • Use of colors and properties
  • Clear purpose

Brainstorming and Planning (15 minutes)

Guide students through planning steps:

  1. Decide what they want to create
  2. Sketch the design
  3. Identify which shapes and colors they will need
  4. Think about layering and order

Teacher demo:

  • Quickly sketch an example on the board
  • Translate the sketch into simple CMU commands

Work Time (40 minutes)

Teacher circulates and supports:

  • Help students break big ideas into small steps
  • Encourage starting simple
  • Remind students to test frequently

Key reminders to students:

  • Build one piece at a time
  • Use the Inspector tool
  • Save work often

Reflection (7 minutes)

Students complete a short written reflection describing:

  • What they built
  • A challenge they faced
  • How Unit 1 concepts were used

Student Tasks

In-Class Tasks

Students will:

  • Brainstorm and plan an original program idea
  • Sketch or outline their design
  • Build the program in CMU CS Academy using Unit 1 tools
  • Write a brief reflection explaining their process

Homework

Students who do not finish must complete:

  • Their Creative Mini-Task program
  • The required reflection component

Checks for Understanding (Formative)

During work time, ask students:

  • "What is the purpose of your program?"
  • "Which Unit 1 concepts are you using?"
  • "How did you decide which shapes to use?"
  • "What problem are you trying to solve right now?"

At the end:

  • Quick share-out of a few student ideas
  • Ask students to self-rate readiness

Common Misconceptions & Fixes

Misconception Fix
Students try to include features not taught Remind them to stay within Unit 1 tools
Students start coding without a plan Require a quick sketch first
Belief that more code automatically means better grade Emphasize quality and rubric
Students get stuck on perfection Encourage incremental progress
Ignoring reflection requirement Remind that reflection is part of grade

Differentiation & Accommodations

For students needing support

  • Provide simple starter templates
  • Suggest easier project ideas
  • Allow pair brainstorming

For advanced students

  • Encourage complex scenes
  • Allow optional advanced creativity
  • Invite them to help peers

General supports

  • Visual planning organizers
  • Checklists of required elements
  • Flexible pacing

Assessment Notes

This lesson results in a graded Creative Mini-Task using the rubric below.

Creative Task Grading Rubric (Unit 1)

Category Strong Evidence (100%) Inconsistent Evidence (70%) Weak/No Evidence (35%)
Program Design
(30%)		 Program clearly demonstrates purpose and incorporates Unit 1 concepts Program shows purpose OR uses Unit 1 concepts, but not both Program does not show purpose or Unit 1 concepts
Program Development
(35%)		 Student identifies a difficulty or opportunity and explains how it was resolved Student identifies a difficulty but does not explain resolution Student does not identify any difficulty
Reflection
(35%)		 Student explains at least two Unit 1 concepts and how they were used Student explains one concept Student cannot explain any concepts

Bonus: Extra points may be awarded for originality, complexity, and creativity.

Closure

"You have moved from learning to follow instructions to creating your own program. That is a huge step as a programmer."

"Finish your project and reflection if needed. Next class we will move on to the next unit and build even more powerful programs."
