Session 2: From Problem → Proposed Solution

Session Length: 180 minutes
Focus: Evaluating existing solutions and proposing a justified technological approach

Big Idea

Good solutions are chosen, not guessed.

Session Overview

Session 2 intentionally mirrors professional workflow. Students research what already exists, compare alternatives, make a reasoned design decision, and document that decision.

By the end of this session, students will understand how existing tools attempt to solve their problem, identify why those solutions fall short, propose a specific solution approach, and justify that approach using research and constraints.

Session 2 Goals (Plain Language)

By the end of Session 2, students will:

  • Understand how existing tools attempt to solve their problem
  • Identify why those solutions fall short
  • Propose a specific solution approach
  • Justify that approach using research and constraints

High-Level Structure

Session 2 intentionally mirrors professional workflow:

  • Research what already exists
  • Compare alternatives
  • Make a reasoned design decision
  • Document the decision

Session 2 Breakdown (180 Minutes)

1

Warm-Up & Framing

Time: 0–15 minutes Format: Instructor-led

Quick reset:

  • "Session 1 = defining the problem"
  • "Session 2 = choosing a solution on purpose"

Emphasize:

  • No solution is perfect
  • Trade-offs are expected
  • Justification matters more than complexity
Optional Quick Prompt (written): "What would a bad solution to your problem look like?"
2

Reading / Research Block: Existing Solutions

Time: 15–45 minutes Format: Individual or Team Research TEKS: 5(A)

Students research current tools, services, or systems that attempt to address their problem.

Guiding questions:

  • What solutions already exist?
  • Who uses them?
  • What do they do well?
  • Where do they fail?
Deliverable (notes): At least 2 existing solutions with pros and cons for each.
3

Writing Block: Comparative Analysis

Time: 45–75 minutes Format: Writing TEKS: 5(B)

Students write a short comparison:

  • Which existing solution is strongest?
  • Why is it still insufficient?
  • What needs remain unmet?

This prevents "reinvent the wheel" thinking.

4

Collaboration Block: Solution Brainstorm

Time: 75–105 minutes Format: Structured peer discussion TEKS: 1(I), 5(C)

In groups:

Students explain their problem + research findings

Peers suggest:

  • Alternative approaches
  • Scope adjustments
  • Risks or concerns

Rules:

  • Solutions must address documented failures
  • No "just make it better" answers
5

Writing Block: Proposed Solution

Time: 105–135 minutes Format: Individual or Team Writing TEKS: 5(B), 6(B)

Students propose:

  • What the solution is
  • How it addresses the problem
  • Why this approach was chosen over others

Still no building.

6

Instructor Feedback #2 (Deeper Conferences)

Time: 135–160 minutes Format: Instructor conferences TEKS: 6(A), 6(F)

You push on:

  • Scope realism
  • User clarity
  • Sustainability
  • Security/privacy concerns
  • Evaluation criteria

This is where you inject industry realism.

7

Reflection & Planning

Time: 160–180 minutes Format: Writing TEKS: 7(A)

Students reflect:

  • How their solution changed after research and feedback
  • What constraints they must respect moving forward
  • What their next step should be

Session 2 Outcomes

By the end of Session 2, students should have:

  • Research-backed understanding of existing solutions
  • A justified proposed solution
  • Clear rationale for design decisions
  • Documentation suitable for a portfolio
