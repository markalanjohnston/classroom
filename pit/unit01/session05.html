Session 5: Refinement & Presentation Preparation

Session Length: 180 minutes
Focus: Refining prototypes and preparing professional presentations

Session Overview

This is your second and final dedicated build session. Today you will complete your MVP prototype, refine your work, and begin preparing for your formal presentation.

By the end of this session, you should have:

  • A completed MVP prototype or mockup
  • Documentation of your work
  • A draft presentation ready for Session 6

How to Work Today

  • This session is self-guided
  • Follow your Session 3 work plan for today
  • Complete remaining MVP features
  • Shift focus to refinement and presentation preparation
  • Work independently or with your approved team
  • Use instructor support when needed

Checkpoint Grade

Your progress today will be assessed as a checkpoint grade. Check your Learning Management System (e.g., Schoology) for specific submission requirements and grading criteria.

You will be graded on:

  • Completion of MVP features
  • Quality of refinement and polish
  • Progress on presentation preparation
  • Documentation of work completed

Session Structure

1

Status Review & Planning

0–10 minutes

Before you begin building:

  • Review your Session 3 work plan for today
  • Assess what you completed in Session 4
  • Identify remaining MVP tasks
  • Determine when to shift from building to refining and presentation prep

Today's goal is to FINISH your MVP, not to add new features.

2

MVP Completion

10–90 minutes

Complete any remaining MVP features from your work plan.

Priorities:

  • Finish core functionality
  • Ensure all "Must Have" features are working
  • Fix critical bugs or errors
  • Test basic functionality

Stop adding features once your MVP is complete. Additional features belong in "Should Have" or "Could Have" categories and can wait.

3

Refinement & Polish

90–130 minutes

Once your MVP is complete, shift focus to refinement.

Refinement Checklist:
  • Test all features thoroughly
  • Fix visual inconsistencies or rough edges
  • Improve labels, text, or instructions for clarity
  • Add visual polish (colors, spacing, layout)
  • Organize files and code
  • Take screenshots or screen recordings of your prototype in action
  • Write clear documentation explaining how it works

This step transforms a working prototype into something you can present professionally.

4

Presentation Preparation

130–175 minutes

Begin preparing your formal presentation for Session 6.

Your Presentation Must Include:

  • Problem: What problem did you identify?
  • Research: What existing solutions did you evaluate?
  • Solution: What did you propose and why?
  • Constraints: What limits did you face?
  • MVP: What did you build?
  • Demonstration: Show your prototype in action
  • Reflection: What did you learn?

Create slides, a script, or visual aids to support your presentation.

Recommended tools: Google Slides, PowerPoint, Canva, Figma

5

Final Documentation & Submission

175–180 minutes

Document your final progress for today.

Your submission must include:

  • Link or files for your completed MVP prototype
  • Screenshots or recordings showing it in action
  • List of completed features
  • Brief description of refinements made
  • Draft presentation materials (slides or outline)

Refer to your Learning Management System (e.g., Schoology) for specific submission requirements.

Finished Early?

If your MVP is complete, refined, and your presentation is drafted:

  • Practice your presentation out loud
  • Create additional visual documentation
  • Add "Should Have" features if time permits
  • Improve your slides or presentation materials
  • Get feedback from peers on your prototype
  • Prepare for potential questions during your presentation

Before You Leave

Make sure you have completed and submitted:

1. Final MVP prototype

2. Progress documentation

3. Draft presentation materials

Check your LMS (e.g., Schoology) to confirm submission requirements.
