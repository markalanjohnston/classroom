Session 6: Formal Presentations & Reflection

Session Length: 180 minutes
Focus: Professional presentations and portfolio finalization

Session Overview

This is the final session of Unit 1. Today you will present your project to the class, demonstrating your problem identification, research, solution design, and MVP prototype.

This session emphasizes professional communication, constructive feedback, and reflective thinking.

By the end of this session, you will have:

  • Delivered a formal presentation
  • Demonstrated your MVP to peers and instructor
  • Received and provided constructive feedback
  • Reflected on your learning and process
  • Finalized portfolio artifacts for Unit 1

Presentation Format

Each student or team will present for 5–7 minutes.

Your presentation must cover:

  • Problem: What problem did you identify and why does it matter?
  • Research: What existing solutions did you evaluate? What did you learn?
  • Solution Proposal: What approach did you choose and why?
  • Constraints: What limits or challenges did you face?
  • MVP Definition: What did you plan to build?
  • Demonstration: Show your prototype or mockup in action
  • Reflection: What worked? What would you do differently?

After your presentation, you will respond to 2–3 questions from peers and the instructor.

Audience Expectations

When you are not presenting, you are expected to:

  • Give your full attention to the presenter
  • Take notes on what you learn from other projects
  • Prepare thoughtful questions
  • Provide constructive feedback
  • Demonstrate professionalism and respect

Your participation as an audience member is part of your grade.

Session Structure

1

Setup & Final Preparation

0–15 minutes

Use this time to:

  • Test your prototype or mockup one final time
  • Load your presentation slides
  • Rehearse your opening
  • Prepare any materials or demos

Technical issues are not an excuse for missing content. Have a backup plan (screenshots, video recording, or detailed description).

2

Formal Presentations

15–140 minutes

Students will present in order. Each presentation includes:

  1. Presentation (5–7 minutes): Present your project following the required structure
  2. Questions (2–3 minutes): Respond to questions from peers and instructor
  3. Transition (1 minute): Next presenter sets up
Feedback Protocol:
  • Ask clarifying questions, not critical ones
  • Focus on what you learned or found interesting
  • Suggest improvements respectfully
  • Avoid making it personal
3

Written Peer Feedback

140–155 minutes

Complete brief written feedback for at least three other projects.

For each project, identify:

  • One strength (what they did well)
  • One area for improvement (constructive suggestion)
  • One thing you learned from their project

This feedback will be shared with the presenters.

4

Individual Reflection

155–170 minutes

Complete a written reflection addressing:

  • Process: What was the most challenging part of this unit?
  • Learning: What did you learn about problem identification, research, and design?
  • Growth: How did your project change from your initial idea to the final MVP?
  • Future: If you continued this project, what would you do next?
  • Skills: What professional skills did you develop or strengthen?

This reflection will be included in your portfolio.

5

Portfolio Finalization

170–180 minutes

Organize all your Unit 1 work into a professional portfolio format.

Your portfolio must include:

  • Session 1: Problem identification document
  • Session 2: Solution proposal and research
  • Session 3: MVP definition and work plan
  • Sessions 4–5: Final prototype/mockup with documentation
  • Session 6: Presentation slides and reflection

Check your Learning Management System (e.g., Schoology) for final submission requirements.

Final Unit 1 Submission

Before the end of class or by the deadline specified in your LMS, submit:

  • Complete portfolio with all Unit 1 artifacts
  • Final presentation slides
  • Individual reflection document
  • Peer feedback forms

Refer to your Learning Management System (e.g., Schoology) for specific submission instructions and due dates.

Unit 1 Complete!

You have completed the full cycle of professional IT project development: from identifying meaningful problems, to researching solutions, to designing and prototyping an MVP, to presenting your work professionally.

These skills and artifacts will serve as the foundation for future units and your professional portfolio.
