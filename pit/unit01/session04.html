Session 4: Design & Prototype Development

Session Length: 180 minutes
Focus: Creating visual or functional representations of the solution

Session Overview

This is your first dedicated build session. You planned for this day during Session 3. Today, you execute that plan.

Your goal is to make visible, measurable progress toward your MVP. By the end of this session, you should have tangible work to show—wireframes, mockups, prototypes, code, diagrams, or other artifacts that move your project forward.

How to Work Today

  • This session is self-guided
  • Follow the work plan you created in Session 3
  • Work independently or with your approved team
  • Focus on creating tangible artifacts
  • Document your progress as you go
  • Use instructor support when needed—ask questions, request feedback, troubleshoot problems

Checkpoint Grade

Your progress today will be assessed as a checkpoint grade. Check your Learning Management System (e.g., Schoology) for specific submission requirements and grading criteria.

You will be graded on:

  • Evidence of productive work time
  • Progress toward your stated MVP
  • Quality and completeness of artifacts created
  • Documentation of work completed

Session Structure

1

Setup & Planning Review

0–10 minutes

Before you begin building:

  • Review your Session 3 work plan
  • Identify which tasks you will complete first
  • Gather necessary tools, accounts, or resources
  • Set up your workspace (digital or physical)

If your plan is unclear or unrealistic, revise it now before you begin.

2

Build Time: Part 1

10–80 minutes

Execute your work plan. Create, build, design, or prototype.

Possible activities include:

  • Creating wireframes or mockups in Figma, Canva, or similar tools
  • Writing code for a prototype or proof-of-concept
  • Designing system diagrams or flowcharts
  • Building data structures or database schemas
  • Drafting user interfaces or interaction flows
  • Setting up development environments or project repositories
Work Expectations:
  • Stay focused on your MVP scope
  • Save your work frequently
  • Document decisions as you make them
  • Ask for help when stuck—don't waste time spinning
3

Progress Check & Adjustment

80–95 minutes

Pause your work and assess your progress.

Answer these questions (write them down):

  • What have you completed so far?
  • What is taking longer than expected?
  • What blockers or problems have you encountered?
  • Do you need to adjust your plan for the rest of today?

Use this time to:

  • Get instructor feedback if needed
  • Troubleshoot technical issues
  • Revise your approach if necessary
4

Build Time: Part 2

95–160 minutes

Continue building. Focus on completing functional components or deliverable artifacts.

Priorities for this block:

  • Complete at least one full feature or visual artifact
  • Make your work presentable (clean up rough drafts)
  • Test functionality if applicable
  • Ensure files are organized and saved properly

Reminder: Done is better than perfect. Focus on creating something tangible, not something flawless.

5

Progress Documentation & Submission

160–180 minutes

Document what you accomplished today.

Your documentation must include:

  • A list of completed tasks
  • Screenshots, links, or files showing your work
  • A brief description of any challenges or changes to your plan
  • A clear statement of what you will do in Session 5
Submission Instructions:

Refer to your Learning Management System (e.g., Schoology) for specific submission requirements, file formats, and due dates.

Finished Early?

If you complete your planned work ahead of schedule:

  • Begin work on Session 5 tasks
  • Add refinement or polish to what you've built
  • Create additional visual documentation
  • Conduct informal user testing with a peer
  • Research solutions to anticipated technical challenges
  • Prepare notes or slides for eventual presentation

Idle time is not acceptable. There is always more you can do to strengthen your project.

Before You Leave

Make sure you have completed and submitted your progress documentation.

Check your LMS (e.g., Schoology) to confirm submission requirements.
