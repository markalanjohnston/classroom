PIT Semester Exam — Career Application & Year-One Review

YOUR ROLE TODAY: Job applicant → New hire → Year-one employee.

A 180-minute professional simulation. You will apply for one of fifteen real-looking entry-level postings, build a portfolio website on the classroom Gitea server, and complete a year-one self-review of the soft skills that matter in every job.

All detailed instructions, the 15 job postings, the rubric, and the submission steps live on the class website. This Schoology page is intentionally brief — the website is your exam booklet for the block.

What You Will Do (High Level)

  1. Step 0 — Choose a posting (15 min): Browse 15 entry-level job postings across software, IT support, healthcare, engineering, business, education, trades, creative, legal, and more. Pick one.
  2. Part 1 — Cover Letter (35 min, 20 pts): One-page tailored cover letter referencing the specific posting and your actual PIT coursework.
  3. Part 2 — Portfolio Website (60 min, 20 pts): Build a single-page portfolio site, push it to a new semester_exam repo on classroom Gitea, confirm it loads publicly. Required sections: hero, about, skills, three artifacts, “why I’m applying,” cover-letter link.
  4. Part 3 — Soft-Skills Self-Review (30 min, 30 pts): Year-one self-review on 5 categories — punctuality, teamwork, communication, professional conduct, adaptability/feedback. Use your performance in this PIT class as the evidence — the semester you just finished is your simulated year one on the job. Self-rate 1–5 with specific class examples and improvement notes.
  5. Part 4 — Technical Reflection (15 min, 15 pts): 2–3 technical skills you actually built, with examples and how they transfer to your chosen job (even if it’s not IT).
  6. Part 5 — Future Goal (10 min, 15 pts): One specific, time-bound career-development goal tied to the posting.
  7. Wrap (15 min): Assemble the PDF, push to Gitea, confirm both deliverables, submit.

Format

  • Individual work — 180-minute block.
  • AI is allowed for brainstorming, drafting, and HTML/CSS first drafts. The final words and the claims you make about yourself must be yours.
  • Mr. Johnston is acting as a roaming supervisor — come with the exact error text, not “it’s not working.”
  • Pace yourself. The website includes a suggested time breakdown for the full block.

Submission — Two Deliverables

1. Portfolio website on Gitea — live at http://10.12.6.236/sites/your-username/semester_exam/. Confirm it loads on a teacher workstation before you call it done.

2. PDF via Schoology — file named LASTNAME_FIRSTNAME_SemesterExam.pdf, containing the cover page, cover letter, soft-skills review, technical reflection, future goal, and a screenshot of your portfolio page loading at the public URL. Use the Submit Assignment button on this page to attach it.

Both must be submitted before the end of the block to earn full credit.

Grading

The exam is evaluated on five criteria. Honest, evidence-based self-evaluation scores highest. Extreme self-ratings may be cross-checked against attendance and submitted work.
