Set up — NO TAPE NEEDED (chairs + signs)

Use the diagram below as your map. You'll need ~24 chairs (you have 30 — plenty).

  1. Place the chairs in the network pattern: a column of 6 chairs on the left for IN, the comparison circles as chairs in the middle columns, and 6 chairs on the right for OUT. Spread it out (~20 ft wide) so two kids fit at each circle.
  2. Drop a printed sign on each chair (from sorting-network-floor-kit.html): IN 1–6 on the left, a COMPARE sign on each middle chair, OUT 1–6 on the right.
  3. Lay the printed ARROW tiles on the floor between chairs to show which paths connect (follow the diagram's lines). Short on time/paper? Skip the arrows — with chairs in the right spots, students just walk to the next chair on the correct side.
  4. Number cards (sorting-network-cards.html — start with the 1–6 set) + a stopwatch (your phone).

The rule (tell the students)

  1. Teams of six — only one team on the network at a time.
  2. Each person takes a number card.
  3. Sit/stand at the six IN chairs in jumbled order (not already sorted!).
  4. Walk along the arrows. When you reach a COMPARE chair, wait for the other person to arrive.
  5. Compare your cards, then take your exit (see the rule below).
  6. Keep going. At the OUT chairs you should be in order, smallest at the top. If you're not — the team starts again!
At each circle: SMALLER number → go to your LEFT  ·  LARGER number → go to your RIGHT.

15 students — heats & jobs

Run heats of 6 (two heats covers 12; rotate so all 15 get a turn). Waiting students get a job:

  • Timekeeper — runs the stopwatch.
  • Recorder — writes the IN order and the OUT order to check it sorted.
  • Node monitors — stand by a COMPARE chair; make sure both people wait and compare correctly.
  • Cheer squad — this is “Beat the Clock,” so make noise!

Beat the Clock: time each heat and challenge them to beat the best time. Reshuffle the numbers between heats.

FOR A SENIOR HELPER Running one heat

  1. Hand out the 6 cards; put the 6 kids at the IN chairs in random order.
  2. Say “Go!” and start the timer.
  3. Remind them: wait at each circle · smaller goes left, larger goes right · walk, don't run.
  4. At OUT, check it's sorted & call the time. Reset, reshuffle, next heat.

Debrief (about 5 min)

  • No matter what order they started in, they came out sorted every time. Why? (The network is built so it always works if everyone follows the rule.)
  • One person sorting alone makes about 12 comparisons in a row. The team finished in only ~5 rounds — because comparisons happened at the same time. That's parallel processing, how multi-core computers go faster.
  • What if someone took the wrong exit? (The answer comes out wrong — computers need exact rules.)
  • Could we sort words or dates with the same network? (Yes — anything you can compare.)

If you have extra time / for tomorrow

  • Reverse it: larger goes left → comes out descending.
  • Harder cards: the 3-digit set, or words compared alphabetically.
  • Challenge: can a small team design a network that sorts just 3 or 4 numbers?

Safety

Walk, don't run. Keep the exit paths clear, and don't slide on loose paper arrows.
