Icebreaker Circle Leader

P-TECH senior · Cafeteria
  1. Get ~10–12 kids into a circle. You're in it too.
  2. Toss the ball to someone. They say their name + answer the question their right thumb is touching, then toss it to someone new.
  3. Keep it moving — about 20–30 seconds each. Shy? "Pass" is fine.
  4. Run out of steam? Use the printed question list.
🎯 Goal: every kid says their name and talks at least once.
🤖

"Harold the Robot" Leader

P-TECH senior · Cafeteria
  1. You (or a brave student) are Harold the Robot. Harold obeys only simple, literal commands: "move hand left," "close hand," "lift up."
  2. Put a few cups/books on the floor. Kids take turns giving one instruction at a time to stack them into a tower.
  3. If a command is too big or fuzzy ("build a tower"), look confused / shake your head — make them break it down.
  4. Swap who plays Harold so everyone laughs.
💬 End with: "Why was that harder than it looks?" → Computers do EXACTLY what you say.
💻

Computer Lab Helper

Senior or AP · Computer Lab
  1. Give each student a login slip → go to academy.cs.cmu.edu → click Login → type the username & password from the slip → open Hour of Code.
  2. Walk the rows. If someone's stuck, don't type for them — ask "What do you want to happen?" and point to the example.
  3. Most common fixes: a missing comma or parenthesis, re-read the step, then click Run.
  4. Paired up? One types, one reads the directions — switch every few minutes.
✅ Keep them on the lesson. Free-draw is the reward when they finish.
📍

Floor Captain

Ms. Sherman / Mr. Johnston
  1. Two rooms: Lab = coding, Cafeteria = games.
  2. Groups A and B swap rooms at ~10:40 (one transition).
  3. Mr. J floats between rooms; seniors lead the small circles/stations.
  4. ≤ 26 kids total? One group — everyone does games first (cafeteria), then coding (lab).
  5. Reconvene in the cafeteria at 11:50 for a quick wrap + Tuesday preview.
⏱ Watch the clock: protect the 10:40 swap and the 11:50 reconvene.
