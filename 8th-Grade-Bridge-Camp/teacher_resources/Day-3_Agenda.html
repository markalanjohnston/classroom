1) Icebreakers & energizers (cafeteria)

Monday's ran short — here's more than you need. Pick what's working and keep moving.

✅ Rock-Paper-Scissors Cheer-Train (the big one — ~8 min, zero prep)

  1. Everyone finds a partner and plays one round of Rock-Paper-Scissors.
  2. The loser becomes the winner's biggest fan — line up behind them, hands on shoulders, chanting their name.
  3. Winners go find another winner and play. The loser and their whole cheering train join behind the new winner.
  4. Keep going until two long trains face off — the whole room chants. One final RPS crowns the camp champion!

Other quick options

  • RPS Evolution: everyone starts as an Egg (waddle). Win a round → evolve: Egg → Chicken (flap) → Dinosaur (roar) → Superhero (pose). Lose → devolve one stage. You can only battle someone at your stage. First to Superhero wins.
  • Find Someone Who (already printed): hand out, mingle, first to 5-in-a-row (or full board) wins.
  • This-or-That line-up: call out pairs (pizza/tacos, summer/winter, cats/dogs, beach/mountains) — kids run to the left or right wall. Fast and loud.

2) Sorting Networks — kinesthetic (cafeteria/gym)

You've got everything printed and you're bringing tape. Tape the network during breakfast, then run heats of 6 and Beat the Clock. The rule, jobs for waiting students, and the debrief are all on your printed run-card; signs + arrows are in the floor kit.

Quick reminder of the rule: at each circle, wait for the other person, compare cards — smaller goes LEFT, larger goes RIGHT — everyone comes out sorted.

3) CMU CS Academy — independent work (lab)

Everyone logs in with their camp account (works for all 15 — that's why we're skipping Minecraft today). Pick up Hour of Code where they left off; finishers do a last-day mini-challenge:

  • Draw your dream room or dream car
  • Make something that moves or animates
  • Design a logo with your initials
  • The silliest scene you can code

Keep it creative and low-pressure — it's the last day.

4) Wrap-up — make it special (last 20 min)

  • Gallery walk: leave your best CMU creation on screen; everyone walks the room and checks out each other's work.
  • Share circle: “favorite part of camp” / “one thing you learned.”
  • Thank them, and tell them what's next for P-TECH. Group photo if they're up for it!
